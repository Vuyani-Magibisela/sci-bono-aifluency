Understanding Conversation Styles

You have successfully set up an account and navigated your way to Microsoft Copilot. You have maybe even fine-tuned your language preferences. Now, you might be wondering: "How do I engage with Copilot effectively?" Well, it all starts with understanding the conversation styles available to you.

Selecting the appropriate conversation style in Copilot helps tailor responses to your specific needs.

At the top of the page, you will find example prompts that show you many ways to engage with Copilot. These prompts are there to spark creativity and to guide you on how to interact with Copilot so you can get the most out of it.

The Three Conversation Styles

As you explore further, you will see options to select a conversation style. You have three distinct styles to choose from:

  • Creative Style: Your ally for ideation, brainstorming, or seeking inspiration. Ideal for creative endeavors like storytelling, drafting emails, or generating new ideas.
  • Balanced Style: Strikes a harmony between informative content and creative expression. Perfect for daily interactions that require both information and a dash of creativity.
  • Precise Style: Your go-to for clear-cut, accurate information. Great for academic research, technical queries, or any context where detail and accuracy are important.

Choosing the Right Style

Each style comes with a brief description to help you understand its use. The style you choose will influence how Copilot responds to your prompts:

  • If you're writing a science fiction story, opt for "more creative"
  • For factual information, "more precise" will serve you well
  • For a mix of both, "more balanced" offers the perfect harmony

Crafting Effective Prompts

Now that you understand the different conversation styles, let's explore how to craft effective prompts that will help you get the most out of Copilot.

Effective prompts provide clear context and specific details to help Copilot generate relevant responses.

Elements of an Effective Prompt

A well-crafted prompt consists of:

  • Clear Objective: What exactly do you need Copilot to do?
  • Relevant Context: Background information that helps Copilot understand the task
  • Specific Details: Parameters, constraints, or preferences that shape the response
  • Desired Format: How you want the information presented (list, table, paragraphs, etc.)

Let's see this in action with some example prompts for each conversation style:

Example Prompts by Style

Creative Style Example:
"Design activities that enhance team spirit and collaboration, featuring a themed event, interactive challenges, and rewards aligned with the company's mission and the diverse interests of the staff."

Precise Style Example:
"Summarize the recent academic research on penguin migration patterns, with a focus on the effects of environmental changes on their travel routes and behaviors."

Balanced Style Example:
"Craft a month-long plan to improve my soft skills at work, outlining specific objectives, enhancement strategies, and progress measurement methods."

Begin by selecting the appropriate conversation style, then type your prompt into the text field provided. If you prefer to speak your prompt, simply click on the microphone icon located to the right of the text field. Once you have entered your prompt, click the submit arrow to continue.

Effective Follow-up Techniques

Upon reviewing the response, you will notice the information is organized according to your prompt. If this is not how you want the information organized, you can refine the results further through follow-up questions or requests.

Follow-up questions and refinement requests help tailor Copilot's responses to your specific needs.

For example, if you want to see the information presented in a table format, go back to the text field and type, "Can you put this into a table?" Since Copilot understands that "this" refers to the previous response, it will generate a table to display the information.

Effective Follow-up Strategies

  • Request Format Changes: "Put this in a table/list/outline format"
  • Ask for More Details: "Can you elaborate on the third point?"
  • Request Simplification: "Explain this in simpler terms"
  • Seek Alternatives: "What are some other approaches I could try?"
  • Ask for Examples: "Can you provide specific examples?"
  • Request Next Steps: "What should I do next based on this information?"

At the bottom right of the interface, you will see a counter indicating how many questions you've asked within the same context (e.g., "2 out of 30"). This means you can ask up to 28 more questions within the same conversation before starting a new topic.

Maintaining Context

Copilot remembers the context of your conversation, allowing you to build on previous exchanges without repeating information. This makes follow-up questions particularly effective, as you can refine and explore a topic in depth through a series of related prompts.

Once done with a conversation, simply click "new topic," and Copilot will be ready to engage with you on a new subject.

Best Practices for Copilot Conversations

To ensure your conversation with Copilot is both engaging and effective, consider these tips:

Following best practices leads to more productive and satisfying interactions with Copilot.

Top Tips for Copilot Conversations

  1. Be Specific: The more details you offer in your prompts, the better Copilot can tailor its response to your needs
  2. Choose the Right Style: Select the conversation style that best matches your current goal
  3. Ask Open-ended Questions: These encourage more detailed and informative responses
  4. Request Additional Details: If a response interests you, ask for more information
  5. Provide Feedback: Let Copilot know if a response doesn't meet your expectations
  6. Embrace Creativity: Ask unique or hypothetical questions—Copilot can handle a broad spectrum of topics
  7. Follow Suggested Topics: Take advantage of related topics provided by Copilot
  8. Maintain Respectful Tone: This ensures a positive interaction

Copilot is here to provide support whenever you need it. Feel free to keep the conversation going, ask questions, and engage—every chat with Copilot helps you learn and discover the world around you.

Practice Makes Perfect

The more you interact with Copilot, the more comfortable you'll become with crafting effective prompts and engaging in productive conversations. Don't be afraid to experiment with different approaches and learn what works best for your specific needs and communication style.

Over time, you'll develop a natural rhythm for working with Copilot, allowing you to harness its full potential as an AI assistant that enhances your productivity, creativity, and learning.
