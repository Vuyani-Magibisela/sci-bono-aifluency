Introduction to Copilot in Edge

Microsoft Edge has taken a significant leap forward by integrating Copilot directly into the browser. This AI feature enhances your browsing experience by allowing you to interact with websites in new and dynamic ways.

Integrating Copilot into Microsoft Edge creates a powerful browsing experience with AI assistance.

Whether you are looking to compose messages, summarize webpage content, or seek assistance with various tasks while browsing, Copilot in Edge is designed to streamline your web interactions. This integration creates a more comprehensive and intuitive experience that goes beyond traditional web browsing.

From Search to AI-Powered Exploration

Copilot in Edge transforms the traditional browsing experience from simply finding information to actively engaging with, analyzing, and creating content based on what you find. This represents a shift from search engines to reasoning engines that understand context and can process information more like a human assistant would.

Getting Microsoft Edge with Copilot

To begin using Copilot in Edge, you first need to download the Microsoft Edge Browser.

Visit the Microsoft Edge website to download the browser with built-in Copilot functionality.

Installation Steps

  1. Visit the official Microsoft Edge webpage at microsoft.com/en-us/edge
  2. At the top of the page, click the "Download" button
  3. On the download page, choose the version compatible with your operating system
  4. Click the "Download" button and follow the on-screen instructions to install the browser
  5. Once installed, Edge will be ready to use with Copilot integration

Once you have Edge installed, accessing Copilot is straightforward:

  1. Open Microsoft Edge Browser
  2. Look for the Copilot icon, typically located in the top right corner of the browser window
  3. Click on the Copilot icon to open the feature within your browser

Key Features of Copilot in Edge

Copilot in Edge offers several features to enhance your browsing experience. Let's explore some of the most useful capabilities:

Copilot in Edge provides a sidebar with multiple tools for browsing enhancement.

Core Capabilities

  • Chat: Allows you to engage in a conversation with Copilot about the content of the webpage you're viewing
  • Compose: Helps you draft messages, emails, or other written content with AI assistance
  • Web Page Summarization: Quickly extracts the key points from lengthy articles or documents
  • PDF Analysis: Analyzes PDF documents to provide summaries, answer questions, or translate content
  • Read Aloud: Converts text to speech for accessibility or multitasking
  • Translation: Translates webpage content or selected text into various languages
  • Screenshot Analysis: Takes and analyzes screenshots for specific information

Copilot in Edge offers several features to enhance your browsing experience. The Chat feature allows you to engage in a conversation with Copilot. The Compose feature can help you draft messages, emails, or even social media posts. Then there are the different conversation styles. Choose from one of the three before you share your prompt.

Practical Examples of Using Copilot in Edge

Let's explore some practical ways to use Copilot in Edge to enhance your browsing experience.

Copilot can help you analyze PDFs with features like summarization, read aloud, and translation.

Example: Analyzing a Webpage

You can follow along by visiting www.microsoft.com/en-us/corporate-responsibility/digital-skills. With the webpage open, click the Copilot icon, colored blue and green, at the top right. If you don't see the Copilot in Edge icon, you might want to check your Edge is running on the latest version and you are signed into your Microsoft account.

Once Copilot in Edge is open, activate the chat feature and select the precise conversation style. Request Copilot to "Provide a summary of this page." Copilot will promptly summarize the content. You can also ask follow-up questions for further clarity, such as "What skilling resources does this page offer?"

For document analysis, Copilot can summarize and translate PDFs within Edge. With a PDF open, click on the "Ask Copilot" button on the top left. Once Copilot is open, you can engage with it by asking questions about the PDF, such as "Provide a summary of the PDF." Copilot will then offer a summary and suggest follow-up questions.

To the right of the "Ask Copilot" button, you will find three black dots. Clicking on them reveals a "Read Aloud" Feature. This feature will read the PDF to you automatically, and you can adjust the reading speed and select a voice type from the "Voice Options" menu.

When in the creative conversation style, you have access to advanced AI capabilities like GPT-4 and DALL-E 3. You can use these for more complex tasks like generating images based on text descriptions or analyzing screenshots for detailed information.
