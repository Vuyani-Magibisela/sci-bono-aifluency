Chapter 5.30: Language and Accessibility Support in Copilot

How Microsoft Copilot breaks down language barriers and enhances accessibility

Language Support Voice Features Visual Search Accessibility

Multilingual Capabilities

Microsoft Copilot is a multilingual AI companion designed to break down language barriers and make access to information easier across the globe. It currently supports an experience in several languages including Simplified Chinese, English, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Brazilian Portuguese, and Spanish.

Microsoft Copilot breaks through language barriers to provide a globally accessible AI experience.

If you encounter an error message indicating that a language isn't supported while using Copilot, it may come as a surprise, particularly if Bing is available in your language. This can happen because language support for Copilot does not extend as far as Bing's language support quite yet.

Currently Supported Languages in Copilot

  • English
  • Chinese (Simplified)
  • French
  • German
  • Italian
  • Japanese
  • Portuguese (Brazil)
  • Spanish

Microsoft continues to expand language support, so this list will grow over time.

Adjusting Language Settings

To adjust language settings in Microsoft Copilot, click the icon with three horizontal lines at the top right corner and select 'Settings'. In the settings menu, navigate to 'language' to set your preferred language for Bing. Here you can also set the language you want Copilot to use for search results and responses.

It's important to note that Copilot currently only accepts prompts in the languages previously mentioned. If you submit a prompt in an unsupported language, Copilot will notify you with an error message.

Voice Capabilities in Copilot

Aside from languages, Copilot was also built with accessibility in mind. Copilot voice is a feature that brings ease and efficiency to your conversations.

Copilot voice enables hands-free interaction through speech recognition and response.

It's perfect for those moments when typing is not the best option, or for users who find speech more accessible than text. To access Copilot voice, simply open Copilot on your device, click the microphone icon, and speak into your device. Copilot will then listen and respond, all in real-time.

Copilot Read Aloud

Copilot read aloud is another accessibility feature that enhances your experience by reading responses aloud. With Copilot open, send an inquiry either through voice or text. Once Copilot provides a response, look for the read aloud icon, which is represented by a speaker image. Click on this icon, and Copilot will read aloud its response.

These voice features make Copilot more accessible to those with visual impairments, reading difficulties, or situations where hands-free operation is necessary. They also provide flexibility in how you interact with the AI, allowing you to choose the method that works best for your current situation.

Visual Search Features

Visual search is another feature that empowers you to explore and understand the world around you. Whether you're curious about a mysterious plant in your garden or the species of a bird perched outside your window, visual search is there to assist.

Visual search in Copilot allows you to identify objects, plants, landmarks, and more using your device's camera.

With a simple image, Copilot becomes your guide, transforming images into knowledge. With Copilot open on your device, tap the camera icon and either upload an image or snap a new one. Next, click the speech bubble to start a conversation about your image. Once you've finished, click the submit icon. Copilot then analyzes the image and provides insights based on your enquiry.

Visual Search Applications

Visual search can be used in numerous ways:

  • Identifying plants, animals, or insects
  • Recognizing landmarks or buildings
  • Reading and translating text in images
  • Understanding artwork or historical artifacts
  • Analyzing diagrams or visual information
  • Finding similar products or items

With the Copilot app, you can snap a photo in a moment and Copilot will analyze it on the spot. Stumbled upon a stunning building and curious about its history? Or maybe you've spotted a rare bird on a walk. Snap a picture, and let Copilot enlighten you with the information you're curious about.

Accessibility Features Overview

Microsoft has designed Copilot with accessibility as a priority, ensuring that its powerful AI capabilities are available to as many people as possible, regardless of abilities or preferences.

Microsoft Copilot incorporates multiple accessibility features to ensure the AI is usable by everyone.

Comprehensive Accessibility Support

Copilot's accessibility features include:

  • Voice Input: Speak your prompts instead of typing
  • Text-to-Speech: Have Copilot read responses aloud
  • Visual Recognition: Get descriptions of images or identify objects
  • Keyboard Navigation: Full keyboard support for people who can't use a mouse
  • Screen Reader Compatibility: Works with screen reading technologies
  • High Contrast Support: Visual elements designed for visibility
  • Language Simplification: Can explain complex topics in simpler terms

To fully leverage Copilot's potential, the best approach is to start using it and share your feedback to Microsoft. This practice not only enhances your experience but also contributes to narrowing the disability divide by helping Microsoft improve accessibility features over time.

Accessibility Beyond Features

Copilot's commitment to accessibility goes beyond technical features. It's designed with an inclusive approach to AI that:

  • Simplifies complex tasks that might otherwise be barriers to technology
  • Provides multiple ways to interact with and receive information
  • Reduces cognitive load by presenting information clearly and concisely
  • Enables personalization to accommodate different needs and preferences

Copilot is not just a tool, it's a step towards an inclusive future where technology serves as a bridge to a world of possibilities, regardless of individual abilities or limitations.
